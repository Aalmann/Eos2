Loops

Loops allow to repeat a sequence of commands. This can be used to simply avoid copy & paste, but it can do a lot more. Loops can be controlled by conditions that determine how long a seequence should be repeated. For example repeat until the next obstacle.

Syntax

Infinite loop

repeat forever
  sequence
*repeat

Counting loop

repeat Number times
  sequence
*repeat

Conditional loop

repeat while condition
  sequence
*repeat

Alternative syntax

while condition do
  sequence
*while

Conditional loop, that runs at elast once

repeat
  sequence
*repeat while condition

Conditional loop with inverted condition

repeat until condition
  sequence
*repeat

Example

c:Circle
repeat 5 times
  c.rotate(1)
*repeat
repeat while c.x < 5
  c.rotate(-1)
*repeat
repeat
  c.rotate(1)    
*repeat while c.y > 10
while c.x < 5 do
  c.rotate(1)
*while
repeat forever 
  c.rotate(2)
*repeat
repeat until c.x < 15
  c.rotate(3)
*repeat

BNF

Loop :=
   "repeat" "forever" Sequence "*repeat" |
   "repeat" "while" Condition Sequence "*repeat" |
   "repeat" "until" Condition Sequence "*repeat" |
   "repeat" Sequence "*repeat" "while" Condition |
   "repeat" Expression "times" Sequence "*repeat" |
   "while" Condition "do" Sequence "*while" |
Sequence := A sequence of commands
